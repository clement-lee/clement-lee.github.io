Lesson

  • Nothing lasts forever
    • Keep up-to-date what’s new
    • Let package management tools take care of the past
  • Future-proof your work
    • It helps others to reproduce your work …
    • … including your future self
    • README helps
  • Adopt early
    • (Ask your PhD student / postdoc to) do it now
    • Doesn’t need to be perfect the first time
    • It will save you time in the future

So far

  • A lot skipped
    • Version control, documentation, package development, testing
    • Container briefly mentioned: binder, docker, etc.
  • Computational reproducibility is only the beginning
    • Social sciences / medical statistics care about related issues
    • Reproducibility? Replicability?
  • Do new data / experiments lead to the same conclusion?
    • Not identical \(p\)-values to what you’ve got (unlikely)
    • Same hypothesis supported / not-supported
  • Does the methodology make sense?
    • Verified results aren’t necessarily good or useful
  • Has the method been changed to fit the result?
    • Garden of the forking paths
